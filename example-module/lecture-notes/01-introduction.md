# Chapter 1: Introduction to the Subject

## 1.1 Key Definitions

A **vector space** \( V \) over a field \( \mathbb{F} \) is a set equipped with two operations:
- **Addition:** \( \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} \in V \) for all \( \mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v} \in V \)
- **Scalar multiplication:** \( \alpha \mathbf{v} \in V \) for all \( \alpha \in \mathbb{F}, \mathbf{v} \in V \)

These operations must satisfy eight axioms (commutativity, associativity, distributivity, etc.).

## 1.2 Basic Properties

**Theorem 1.1.** The zero vector \( \mathbf{0} \) is unique.

*Proof.* Suppose \( \mathbf{0} \) and \( \mathbf{0}' \) are both zero vectors. Then:
\[
\mathbf{0} = \mathbf{0} + \mathbf{0}' = \mathbf{0}' + \mathbf{0} = \mathbf{0}'
\]
Hence \( \mathbf{0} = \mathbf{0}' \). \( \square \)

## 1.3 Examples

1. \( \mathbb{R}^n \) with componentwise addition and scalar multiplication.
2. The set of polynomials of degree \( \leq n \).
3. The set of \( m \times n \) matrices over \( \mathbb{R} \).
